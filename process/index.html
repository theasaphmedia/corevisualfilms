How We Work

Our Process

Every great film starts before a camera is turned on. Here's how we approach every project, from the first email to final delivery.

01

Discovery

The Conversation

Before anything else, we listen. A 30-minute call or meeting where we try to understand not just what you want, but why you want it — and what the film needs to do in the world. We ask the questions no brief ever asks.

This conversation shapes everything that follows. We'd rather turn down a project than start without understanding it.

Free initial consultation30–60 min callNo obligationWithin 24 hours
02

Pre-Production

The Blueprint

Once we're aligned, we build the production plan: locations, shot lists, call sheets, gear lists, crew, and a creative brief that everyone can point to. No surprises on shoot day.

For larger projects, this includes storyboards, mood boards, and a detailed timeline with clear milestones and approval gates.

Creative briefShot listLocation scoutingCrew coordinationMood boards
03

Production

The Shoot

Shoot day. We arrive over-prepared and stay flexible. Every frame is motivated. We move fast when we need to and slow down when something worth capturing is happening in the room.

We are obsessively quiet on set. Not because we're distant — but because the best moments require silence around them to breathe.

Dual camera where neededDrone aerialsNatural light prioritisedAudio capture
04

Post-Production

The Edit

Where the film is actually made. Our editors build a first cut, then we refine it together through a structured feedback process. Two rounds of revisions are included as standard. The final cut goes to colour grade and sound mix.

We colour in DaVinci Resolve with a custom LUT developed over years of work. The grade isn't a filter — it's a decision about how this specific story should feel.

2 revision roundsDaVinci Resolve gradeSound designLicensed music
05

Delivery

The Handover

Delivery in every format you need: broadcast masters, web-optimised H.264, social crops, and an archived ProRes copy. We don't disappear after delivery — questions about how to share, embed, or use the film are always welcome.
